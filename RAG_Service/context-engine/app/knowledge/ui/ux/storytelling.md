# Storytelling in UI/UX Design

UX design, or User Experience design, focuses on the experience your customers have as they move through your product. This is no different in concept than physical interior design: how a customer enters, walks through, and leaves a space.

---

## The Customer Journey
Start with the customer journey map. Consider the questions a customer asks themselves upon entering your digital space:
* Will I know where to go?
* Is this website/app the right place for me?
* What is this product?
* Who can I ask for help?
* How long will this take?
* Can I trust this brand?

These are all elements of their journey and should be considered when designing a solution, particularly the **order** in which they arrive at these answers and how smoothly they can progress to the next step.

---

## Key Narratives in Design

### 1. Welcoming the User
Books begin by bringing the reader into the experience—setting the stage, introducing characters, and establishing lives. If the reader doesn't feel engaged here, they won't keep reading.
* Welcome your customers with common courtesies.
* Introduce yourself and what you are offering immediately (e.g. "Find and book hotels in Nigeria").
* Treat the digital space like your home: you want guests to enter, feel welcome, stay a while, and leave knowing they had a good time.

### 2. Narrative Progression (E-Commerce Example)
Rather than throwing all information at the user at once, organize it as a simple, comfortable path through your story:
1. **Introduction**: Logo, site name, value proposition, and welcome.
2. **Category offerings**: Representing the collection of options.
3. **Exploration path**: Selecting a style, customizing color/size, and reviewing choices.
4. **Action**: Adding to cart, checkout, and post-purchase follow-up.

### 3. Progressive Disclosure
You do not need to arrange all of this information on a single screen. Otherwise, it becomes overwhelming.
* Let the experience unfold over time.
* Reveal information (like security details or shipping speeds) only when relevant—exactly when the user needs it to make the next choice.
* Design is just as much about the things you choose **not** to include as the things you do.

---

## Why Storytelling Matters to Teams
Crafting a narrative around your products is not just helpful for customer satisfaction; it is crucial to **defining and defending your design decisions** to teammates, clients, and stakeholders.
* Explaining the narrative progression makes design decisions easier to grasp.
* Sharing the story builds customer empathy and validates assumptions.
* Site maps and storyboards visualize key moments to unify development teams.
